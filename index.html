A Brief History

The history of water slides can be traced back to early water parks and theme parks in the 20th century. The first documented water slide was presented in New Zealand at the 1906 International Exhibition, featuring "chute boats" that slid into a lake. Herbert Sellner, in 1923, designed a similar ride with water toboggans in Minnesota. George Millay later popularized the concept of water parks, opening the first SeaWorld in San Diego in 1964 and the first dedicated water park, Wet \'n Wild, in Orlando in 1977

#1-Insano

Description

The Insano is a body water slide at Beach Park in Aquiraz, Brazil, known for being the tallest freestanding body slide in the world. It is 41 meters (135 feet) tall, equivalent to a 14-story building. Riders experience a rapid descent and high speeds, reaching up to 105 km/h (65 mph).

#2-Thrillagascar & Jungle Jammer

Description

Thrillagascar and Jungle Jammer are two high-speed, trapdoor body slides located at DreamWorks Water Park in East Rutherford, New Jersey, USA. They are known for being the world's tallest indoor water slides, standing 142 feet (14 stories) tall. Riders experience free fall speeds as they are launched from the trapdoor capsules.

#3-Summit Plummet

Description

Summit Plummet at Disney's Blizzard Beach is a high-speed, near-vertical body slide that plunges riders 12 stories down a 360-foot run, reaching speeds of up to 60 miles per hour. The ride is a free-fall, sending riders through a darkened tunnel before emerging into a massive spray of whitewater. It's known for its extreme thrills and is considered the most extreme attraction at Blizzard Beach.

#4-Leap of Faith

Description

The Leap of Faith is a thrilling waterslide at Atlantis Paradise Island in the Bahamas, part of the Aquaventure Waterpark. This slide features an almost-vertical drop of 60 feet from the top of the Mayan Temple. Riders plummet through a clear, acrylic tunnel submerged in a lagoon teeming with marine life, including sharks, according to Atlantis Bahamas.

#5-Kilmanjaro

Description

The Kilimanjaro is a water slide at Aldeia das Aguas Park Resort in Barra do Pirai, Brazil, known for its 164-foot drop height and reaching speeds of up to 62 mph. It was the world's tallest water slide for over 12 years. The slide is named after Africa's tallest mountain, Mount Kilimanjaro.

#6-Captain Spacemaker

Description

Captain Spacemaker is a high-thrill water slide at Caribe Bay in Italy, known for its extreme height and steep descent. It's a mega-water slide where riders experience a 60-degree launch at a 42-meter launch, reaching speeds of up to 100 km/h. It is considered the tallest water slide in Europe.

#7-Meteor

Description

A meteor waterslide is a type of water slide that typically features a high drop, simulating a free fall, with a steep incline and a rapid descent. These slides are known for their high speed and adrenaline rush, often reaching speeds of over 100 km/h. #8-Deep Water Dive

Description

The Deep Water Dive at Kentucky Kingdom Theme & Water Park is a high-speed, body slide that drops riders 121 feet down a 70-degree angle. It's 377 feet long and, at one point, was described as the tallest body slide in North America, though this was later revealed to be an April Fool's Day joke.

#9-Verti-go

Description

Verti-Go at Aqualandia Benidorm is a twin slide attraction featuring the world's highest capsule slide and the highest slide in Europe. The two slides, a 28-meter and a 33-meter slide, offer a freefall experience with speeds exceeding 100 km/h and a gravitational acceleration dependent on rider mass. The 33-meter slide, the tallest capsule slide, is also the highest slide in Europe.

#10-Scorpions Tail

Description

Scorpion's Tail at Noah's Ark Waterpark is a high-speed, nearly vertical looping water slide. Riders drop from a trapdoor, plummeting down the slide before entering an almost vertical loop and splashing down into a funnel. Ten stories high, 400-feet long, and initial drop that sends you plummeting down at more than 50-feet per second.
